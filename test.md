# Gaters Systems Draft

## Design pillars

- **Reach equals exposure** — growth requires opening outward; opening outward creates risk.
- **Bodies die, players persist** — avatars, gear, homes, holdings, and empires can be lost; player existence cannot.
- **Safety is obscurity** — a quiet home is hard to locate, not invulnerable.
- **No defender tax** — absence must not create routine loss, decay, or chores.
- **Finite Gates drive conflict** — Gates are found, claimed, repaired, and fought over; players never manufacture them.
- **The Gate is the seam** — travel, trade, raiding, claiming, discovery, and exposure all route through Gates.
- **Explain rules, not the soul** — avatar continuity works operationally; the metaphysics stay part of the Builder mystery.

## Player requirements

### Served

- **Raider**
  - **RAIDER-1 Find targets** — raiders need ways to locate valuable targets.
  - **RAIDER-2 Breach** — raids need a bounded fight, not wall math.
  - **RAIDER-3 Loot** — targets must hold value worth the risk.
  - **RAIDER-4 Extract** — raiders need a way home with stolen goods.
  - **RAIDER-5 Anonymity** — raiders need peer-facing anonymity until commitment.

- **Builder**
  - **BUILDER-1 Safe home** — builders need long-term homes without decay or chores.
  - **BUILDER-2 Build depth** — structures, defenses, production, and layout must matter.
  - **BUILDER-3 Fair defense** — defense happens around the perimeter, not doorstep cheese.
  - **BUILDER-4 Exposed building** — forward bases and holdings need upside worth the risk.

- **Explorer**
  - **EXPLORER-1 Unknown frontier** — explorers need unknown Gates/worlds to discover.
  - **EXPLORER-2 Valuable intel** — coordinates, routes, traces, and signatures must be useful loot.
  - **EXPLORER-3 Compounding maps** — early scouting should become late-game leverage.
  - **EXPLORER-4 Blind dialing** — random frontier discovery must work without target intel.

- **Trader**
  - **TRADER-1 Resource asymmetry** — worlds need different outputs so goods move.
  - **TRADER-2 Routes** — traders need hubs, direct dials, access lists, and markets.
  - **TRADER-3 Cargo risk** — trade should risk cargo without always risking the whole home.
  - **TRADER-4 Sinks** — tolls, tithe, fuel, and fees keep trade meaningful.

- **Conqueror**
  - **CONQUEROR-1 Holdings** — exposed assets must be worth taking.
  - **CONQUEROR-2 Capture** — conquest needs take-and-hold mechanics.
  - **CONQUEROR-3 Peer targets** — conquest should aim at peer-or-greater rivals.
  - **CONQUEROR-4 Mortal empire** — empires can fall; players persist.

- **Homesteader**
  - **HOMESTEAD-1 Quiet safety** — quiet players should be hard to locate.
  - **HOMESTEAD-2 No chores** — long-term building should not require upkeep babysitting.
  - **HOMESTEAD-3 Soft ceiling** — staying quiet should progress for a long time, then plateau.
  - **HOMESTEAD-4 Solo PvE** — frontier PvE should remain valuable without forcing PvP.

- **Tamer**
  - **TAME-1 Frontier creatures** — creatures should come from frontier worlds.
  - **TAME-2 Narrow depth** — taming exists; deep ARK-style breeding/upkeep does not dominate.

### Redirected

- **PvP brawler**
  - **BRAWLER-1 Quick combat** — combat-focused players need fast fights.
  - **BRAWLER-2 Arena surface** — brawling belongs on contested frontier worlds, space Gates, or other always-live surfaces.

- **Clan / zerg**
  - **GROUP-1 Large objectives** — large groups need objectives worth coordinating around.
  - **GROUP-2 No punch-down** — large groups must not easily farm solos/newbies.
  - **GROUP-3 Group payoff** — group play needs rewards that justify organization overhead.

### Denied

- **Offline raider as default**
  - **DENY-OFFLINE-1 Rare offline raids** — homes can be raided if located, but offline raiding must not be routine.
  - **DENY-OFFLINE-2 No sleeping body** — the player’s real body is not in-world as an offline target.

- **Megabase newbie farmer**
  - **DENY-WHALE-1 Obscurity wall** — strong players should not easily find small quiet homes.
  - **DENY-WHALE-2 Uphill visibility** — frontier visibility should keep whales away from low-power targets.

- **Turtle / babysitter**
  - **DENY-TURTLE-1 No permanent bunker meta** — staying quiet is safe but capped.
  - **DENY-TURTLE-2 No obligation** — players should never feel forced to log in to prevent decay/loss.

## Failure modes

- **TURTLE-1 Turtle equilibrium** — if quiet play is optimal forever, everyone bunkers.
  - Counter: quiet play has a soft ceiling; growth needs exposure.

- **TRADE-SUICIDE-1 Trade-suicide** — if trade exposes the vault like raiding, nobody trades.
  - Counter: open; needs a cleaner trade-risk model.

- **OFFLINE-DODGE-1 Offline dodge** — instant closing mid-raid cancels raiding.
  - Counter: committed tunnels, siege clocks, and finite engagements.

- **ZERG-1 Zerg pile-on** — if opening exposes you to everyone, mass always wins.
  - Counter: bilateral directional tunnels and visibility limits.

- **WHALE-1 Newbie farming** — if large houses can locate small houses easily, retention dies.
  - Counter: obscurity plus uphill frontier visibility.

- **DOORSTEP-1 Doorstep kill-box** — if the Gate can be walled in, raids become spawn cheese.
  - Counter: Gate dome clears the aperture.

- **PERMA-LEAK-1 Permanent coordinate leak** — if one leak lasts forever, one mistake dooms a home.
  - Counter: stale intel, address rotation, decay, or another recovery valve.

## World premise

- **The Builders were dying** — their species faced biological collapse.
- **They had machines and AI** — but could not prove machine/upload continuity preserved real consciousness.
- **They launched von Neumann probes** — probes seeded worlds, built Gates, studied life, and searched for durable biological consciousness.
- **The Gates arrived too late** — Gates only connect completed endpoints; probes took too long to build escape routes.
- **The Builders died** — the machine continued after them.
- **The Gate network is a research machine** — transport, testing, claiming, vaulting, and observation are all one system.
- **Earth may be a wild result** — a probe may have found Earth too early and marked it immature.
- **The Wake** — the network noticed unseeded consciousness when humans activated or disturbed the dormant Earth Gate.
- **The Builder treasure exists** — deeper Builder research is locked behind survival, claim, adaptation, and consciousness tests.

## Gates

- **GATE-1 Finite Gates** — Gates are found, claimed, repaired, and fought over; players never manufacture them.
  - Serves: RAIDER-3, EXPLORER-1, CONQUEROR-1.
  - Prevents: TURTLE-1.

- **GATE-2 Closed** — no active tunnel exists.
  - Closing ends current contact.
  - Closing does not erase enemy intel.

- **GATE-3 Open** — one active tunnel exists.
  - Travel, trade, raids, and exposure happen through open Gates.

- **GATE-4 One live tunnel** — one ring holds one active tunnel at a time.
  - Serves: RAIDER-2, BUILDER-3.
  - Prevents: ZERG-1.

- **GATE-5 Directional dialing** — the dialing side opens the tunnel and pays the power.
  - Serves: RAIDER-2, RAIDER-4.
  - Test: can the attacker commit without turning every opening into a public pile-on?

- **GATE-6 Dial out** — spend power to reach an unknown frontier Gate.
  - Serves: EXPLORER-1, EXPLORER-4, HOMESTEAD-4.

- **GATE-7 Dial known** — open a tunnel to a saved coordinate.
  - Serves: TRADER-2, RAIDER-1, CONQUEROR-1.

- **GATE-8 Dome** — each Gate projects a close protective dome around the aperture.
  - The dome prevents building, roofing, burying, walling, or kill-boxing the Gate mouth.
  - Serves: RAIDER-2, BUILDER-3.
  - Prevents: DOORSTEP-1.
  - Line: you can own a Gate; you cannot silence it.

- **GATE-9 Field** — each claimed/active Gate projects a sustaining field.
  - The field powers masks and creates the soft world border.
  - Serves: RAIDER-2, HOMESTEAD-4, BUILDER-1.

## Coordinate knowledge

- **COORD-1 Unknown** — another player does not have enough current intel to dial you.
  - Serves: BUILDER-1, HOMESTEAD-1.
  - Prevents: WHALE-1.

- **COORD-2 Traced** — another player has fragments, routes, signatures, shares, or clues.
  - Serves: RAIDER-1, EXPLORER-2.

- **COORD-3 Located** — another player has enough current intel to dial you.
  - Serves: RAIDER-1, CONQUEROR-1.
  - Risks: DENY-OFFLINE-1.

- **COORD-4 Stale** — old intel no longer completes a dial.
  - Serves: BUILDER-1, HOMESTEAD-1.
  - Prevents: PERMA-LEAK-1.

- **COORD-5 Coordinate database** — a homebase record of known routes and coordinates.
  - The database is a raidable asset.
  - Serves: RAIDER-3, EXPLORER-2, TRADER-2.

- **COORD-6 Address recovery valve** — old home intel must be able to go stale somehow.
  - Method open: address rotation, heat decay, sample lifetime, stale keys, or relocation.
  - Serves: BUILDER-1, HOMESTEAD-1.
  - Prevents: PERMA-LEAK-1.
  - Risks: TURTLE-1 if too cheap.

## Exposure

- **EXPOSE-1 Activity creates traces** — opening Gates, moving mass, fighting, claiming, trading, and repeated routing leave evidence.
  - Serves: RAIDER-1, EXPLORER-2.
  - Prevents: TURTLE-1.
  - Risks: TRADE-SUICIDE-1.

- **EXPOSE-2 Quiet play leaks less** — closed, small, low-traffic homes are hard to locate.
  - Serves: BUILDER-1, HOMESTEAD-1.
  - Prevents: WHALE-1.

- **EXPOSE-3 Big houses leak more** — more members, routes, masks, cargo, and claims create more traces.
  - Serves: RAIDER-1, CONQUEROR-3.
  - Prevents: DENY-WHALE-1.

- **EXPOSE-4 Claimed homes are not in the random frontier pool** — blind dialing never drops players onto occupied soil.
  - Serves: BUILDER-1, HOMESTEAD-1, EXPLORER-4.
  - Prevents: WHALE-1.

## Avatar & mask

- **AVATAR-1 Body dies, gater returns** — the field body is mortal; the registered gater persists.
  - Serves: RAIDER-3, CONQUEROR-4, DENY-OFFLINE-2.

- **AVATAR-2 Metaphysics unresolved** — nobody fully knows whether continuity is transfer, projection, copying, succession, or something stranger.
  - The Coalition certifies continuity; it does not explain it.

- **AVATAR-3 Biological body** — the avatar is living biological substrate, not a robot.
  - Serves: CLAIM-1, TAME-1.
  - Prevents: robot replacement.

- **AVATAR-4 One active body** — a gater may have a roster, but only one active field body at a time.
  - Serves: RAIDER-2, BUILDER-3.
  - Prevents: multibox pressure.

- **MASK-1 Required interface** — the mask is required for a body to function as a gater avatar.
  - The mask is life support, HUD, scanner, claim key, sync buffer, field battery, coordinate imprint, and black box.

- **MASK-2 Mask energy** — mask energy is health, stability, and away timer.
  - Damage drains mask energy.
  - Away from Gate fields, the mask runs on reserve.
  - Serves: RAIDER-2, RAIDER-4, HOMESTEAD-4.

- **MASK-3 Field dependency** — home/friendly Gate fields sustain the mask; hostile/foreign fields may only partially sustain it.
  - Serves: RAIDER-2, BUILDER-1.
  - Test: does hostile soil create a real clock without feeling arbitrary?

- **MASK-4 Scanner/HUD** — the mask is the in-world interface.
  - Shows health, reserve, field strength, compass, scanner results, Gate diagnostics, claim progress, route data, comms, alerts, and sync status.
  - Serves: EXPLORER-2, TRADER-2, RAIDER-1.

- **MASK-5 Anchor imprint** — fielded masks carry enough home/house imprint to return, sync, and recognize full sustain.
  - This is the lore basis for mask-derived exposure.
  - Serves: RAIDER-1, EXPLORER-2.
  - Risks: TRADE-SUICIDE-1, PERMA-LEAK-1.

## Claiming

- **CLAIM-1 Consciousness test** — claiming is a test of living agency disguised as ownership.
  - Serves: CONQUEROR-1, EXPLORER-1.
  - Prevents: robot replacement.

- **CLAIM-2 Blood + mask + intent** — blood proves living substrate, mask proves recognized continuity, intent proves agency.
  - Line: a claim is written in blood and keyed by the mask.

- **CLAIM-3 Gate registers claim patterns** — the Gate does not understand human property law.
  - Serves: CONQUEROR-2, BUILDER-1.

- **CLAIM-4 Coalition registers legal ownership** — the Coalition maps Gate claim patterns into house ownership, permissions, standing, and tithe.
  - Serves: TRADER-2, GROUP-3, CONQUEROR-2.

## Houses

- **HOUSE-1 Solo is house of one** — the same ownership model serves solos and groups.
  - Serves: BUILDER-1, GROUP-3.

- **HOUSE-2 Shared claim** — a house is several claimants bound into one shared claim pattern.
  - Serves: GROUP-1, CONQUEROR-2.

- **HOUSE-3 Roles and permissions** — house members should not all have equal destructive power.
  - Serves: GROUP-3, BUILDER-1.
  - Prevents: internal grief deleting a house with one button.

- **HOUSE-4 Bigger houses are leakier** — more fielded members and routes create more exposure.
  - Serves: RAIDER-1, CONQUEROR-3.
  - Prevents: megahouses becoming invisible.

- **HOUSE-5 Dead houses** — abandoned or failed claims seed PvE raid content.
  - Serves: RAIDER-3, HOMESTEAD-4, EXPLORER-1.
  - Test: dead-house loot must train the raid loop without replacing player raids.

## Combat & raids

- **COMBAT-1 Aperture fight** — raiding begins at the Gate dome, not a loading screen.
  - Serves: RAIDER-2, BUILDER-3.
  - Prevents: DOORSTEP-1.

- **COMBAT-2 Away clock** — attackers operate under mask reserve on hostile soil.
  - Serves: RAIDER-2, BUILDER-3.
  - Test: can a skilled attacker breach and loot without camping forever?

- **COMBAT-3 Defender perimeter** — defenders fortify around the dome, not directly on the aperture.
  - Serves: BUILDER-3.
  - Prevents: DOORSTEP-1.

- **COMBAT-4 Muster cap** — the dome limits how many combat-effective defenders can press the aperture.
  - Serves: RAIDER-2, GROUP-2.
  - Prevents: ZERG-1.

- **COMBAT-5 Extraction** — raiders must withdraw through their route before reserve/pressure collapses.
  - Serves: RAIDER-4.

## Economy & trade

- **ECON-1 Resource asymmetry** — worlds have different outputs.
  - Serves: TRADER-1, EXPLORER-1.

- **ECON-2 Stagnation ceiling** — quiet play progresses, then plateaus.
  - Serves: HOMESTEAD-3.
  - Prevents: TURTLE-1.

- **ECON-3 Power cores/fuel** — dialing, distance, claims, and routes consume meaningful resources.
  - Serves: TRADER-4, RAIDER-3.

- **TRADE-1 Trade risk model open** — trade needs cargo risk without always risking the home vault.
  - Serves: TRADER-3.
  - Risks: TRADE-SUICIDE-1.

- **HUB-1 Hubs** — hubs route trade, reduce distance costs, and create toll/capture targets.
  - Serves: TRADER-2, CONQUEROR-1, GROUP-1.

## Progression

- **PROG-1 Potential** — a sticky strength rating drives visibility, tiering, and fair matchmaking.
  - Serves: CONQUEROR-3, GROUP-2.
  - Prevents: WHALE-1.

- **PROG-2 Mind-XP** — persistent knowledge, discoveries, adaptations, and high-water marks survive.
  - Serves: AVATAR-1, HOMESTEAD-3.

- **PROG-3 Avatar-XP** — body-local growth and proficiency can die with the avatar.
  - Serves: RAIDER-3.
  - Test: death hurts without feeling like account deletion.

- **PROG-4 Frontier ladder** — surface worlds lead to richer worlds, hubs, space Gates, and Supergates.
  - Serves: EXPLORER-1, GROUP-1, CONQUEROR-1.

## Open questions

- **OPEN-1 Avatar origin** — exact body source and continuity mechanics remain unresolved by design.
- **OPEN-2 Trade risk** — trade must not expose the home exactly like raiding.
- **OPEN-3 Exposure model** — choose between avatar-shares, Gate heat/samples, or a simpler hybrid.
- **OPEN-4 Address recovery** — decide how old location data becomes stale without enabling free safety.
- **OPEN-5 House permissions** — decide who can re-key, claim, deploy, invite, recall, or bind Gates.
- **OPEN-6 Mask specifics** — decide field gradient, away reserve, hostile sustain, and scanner scope.
- **OPEN-7 Return home after wipe** — decide where recall lands if the home anchor is destroyed.
- **OPEN-8 Builder truth reveal** — keep the purpose of the machine hidden until payoff earns it.

## Current language rules

- Use **Closed/Open** for physical Gate connection state.
- Use **Unknown/Traced/Located/Stale** for coordinate knowledge.
- Avoid **sealed** unless it only means ancient dormant Builder state.
- Use **Dial out** for random frontier exploration.
- Use **Dial known** for saved coordinates.
- Use **Located** instead of **found** when precision matters.
- Use **house** for the ownership unit; solo players are houses of one.
- Use **Serves / Prevents / Risks / Test** under mechanics when needed.
