# Gate availability: Authority-certified provisioning

Not every Gate is dialable at a given moment. Availability has two layers.

**Demand-driven dialing (the default, everywhere).** A world is an empty database row
until a player spends a power core and supplies its coordinates to dial it — dialing
*is* provisioning, so a server instance exists only while someone pays to sustain the
world (Empty → Solo-occupied → Contested, then torn back down to a row). This is how
the **frontier** works: direct point-to-point dialing (the mesh of DDR-0004) on
coordinates you discover, with no Authority involvement — its writ stops there.

**Authority certification (the published core).** The [[Central Authority]] certifies
regions as the network stabilises after [[The Wake]] and publishes their lanes; the
certified-region list is a server-side allowlist the studio controls. It is the lever
for staged rollout, core capacity, and content drops delivered diegetically as
Directorate bulletins — which locks "Authority as Patch Channel" as canon (resolves
open-question #14). The Safe Core is always certified; new world tiers ship as new
certifications.

One invariant keeps it safe: certification and provisioning govern reaching **others'**
and **new** worlds, never your own home Gate — your sealed planet-base is always
dialable by you, so the no-offline-loss guarantee (DDR-0001, reach equals exposure) is
untouched.

Chosen over pure demand-driven dialing alone (gives the studio no lever over rollout,
core capacity, or content cadence) and over per-Gate stability *windows* (the strongest
capacity lever, but it pulls players onto the network's schedule — eroding the
no-obligation thesis — and double-books the Rift cosmology; held for frontier worlds
later). Presumes the nexus / mesh-with-a-heart of DDR-0004.
