# Gates × PvP / PvE / PvPvE — review and Builder-tech options

Filed research report, 2026-07-02. Reviews what the canon currently intends for the
Gate as the PvP/PvE/PvPvE seam, then offers Builder-plausible gate-technology options —
including how AI actors (dead houses) can populate every contest surface. Options only;
nothing here is canon until folded in on request.

## Part 1 — Review: what the canon intends today

### The map (all three modes already route through the Gate)

- **PvE**: sealed home solo (build/explore, cheap tick, no decay) → frontier solo
  (dial out under the away-reserve leash, push-your-luck) → dead-house raids
  (HOUSE-2: an AI base is a house on the same code path — PvE that trains the raid loop).
- **PvP**: home raid (bilateral directional tunnel, gated by finding shares, attacker's
  away reserve as the clock, dome breach, muster cap) · hub capture (registry flip held
  by presence) · holdings contest (flow-tap vs. a frozen-snapshot AI defence).
- **PvPvE**: the contested frontier world (FRONTIER-1/3 — multi-gate worlds, sequential
  dials, planet runtime states row → solo → contested), flavoured by biome tags,
  monster-spill, and dead-house husks.

### What holds up — bank it, don't re-solve it

- **One cosmology.** Every surface _is_ the Gate: one travel phenomenon, one instancing
  model, one combat model, one ownership primitive (house), and AI bases on the same
  code path as player bases. This discipline is rare and is the design's best asset.
- **The clocks compose.** The away reserve is simultaneously raid clock, combat
  durability, extraction timer, and instance-lifetime bound — four bills, one number.
- **The guardrails are structural, not bolted on.** Anti-zerg is the bilateral tunnel;
  anti-whale is uphill visibility; anti-kill-box is the dome. Fixes below must not
  weaken any of them.

### Gaps, ranked

1. **Trade risk (questions #2) — the biggest named hole.** Binary sealed/open leaves the
   trade-suicide trap uncountered and the Trader archetype starving. The transponder
   (USE-1, option bank) is the closest existing lever; see option A.
2. **PvPvE has arrivals but no escalation.** FRONTIER-3 gets multiple parties _to_ a
   world; once there, nothing ramps. The E is static (creatures + husk defences), no
   mechanic makes a fight hotter, draws crashers, or ends it other than each party's
   private clock — and third-party discovery of an in-progress contest is only "the
   world is listed." See options C and D.
3. **AI actors are defensive-only.** Dead houses defend a frozen snapshot; no AI ever
   dials, pushes, hauls, or arrives as a third force. At low population — the greybox's
   own audience — hub capture, holdings contest, the extraction race, and the muster cap
   have nothing exercising them. HOUSE-2 keeps the _attack-a-base_ half alive at low
   pop; the _meet-someone-out-there_ half is dead. See option E.
4. **Matchmaking/visibility rules have no in-world carrier.** Bilateral-tunnel
   matchmaking is open (Raiding); uphill-only reach, Potential-gated visibility, and hub
   concurrency caps are bare game rules. The chronology-protection lever
   ([[gate-physics]]) is parked and unassigned — the one Builder safety already grounded
   in real physics. See option B.

### Standing trap-checks on any addition

- Nothing may punish an absent player — AI raiding a found home offline is decay reborn.
- A hub/frontier surface may lower _travel cost_ to a doorstep, never the _target's
  defences_.
- Never a defender-side timer or scheduled window (rejected calls stand: SIEGE-1,
  Holdings).

## Part 2 — Options: Builder-plausible gate tech

Each option names what it adds, the upstream knob that carries it, the plausibility
anchor, and its trap-check. Ladder-checked: most are adoptions/retunes of existing
option-bank entries or parked levers, not new systems. Tunables named, values to data.

### A. Adopt the transponder handshake (USE-1) — [exists, leaning-in]

- Trade dial presents an accepted code; a raid dial is anonymous until commit; spoofing
  a code is an infiltration play. The gate protocol itself distinguishes "open for
  business" from "open to attack."
- Plausibility: a mesh of thousands of mouths needs a handshake layer; humans half-decode
  it, which is why spoofing exists.
- Doesn't decide #2 (binary exposure is a recorded call — do not silently change it),
  but it's the mechanism most #2 option paths share; adopting it early is low-regret.
- Tunables: code-acceptance scope, spoof difficulty/cost.

### B. Assign the chronology-protection safeties to carry matchmaking/visibility — [parked lever]

- The network refuses or collapses dials that would destabilise the mesh (Visser Roman
  ring / Hawking conjecture — already GROUNDED-tagged and parked in [[gate-physics]]).
  Let that one Builder safety diegetically carry: uphill-only reach, Potential-gated
  frontier visibility, hub concurrency caps, and why some dials simply fail.
- PvP payoff: bilateral-tunnel matchmaking stops being a lobby system and becomes "the
  network won't sustain that lane" — one safety explains four rules that are currently
  bare.
- Plausibility: the strongest available — Builder engineering wrapped around a real
  conjecture; STRETCH when used as a mechanic, per the existing tag.
- Trap-check: clean; it _is_ the guardrails, restated in-world.

### C. Strain flare — contested worlds get louder — [retune of existing canon]

- Canon already says active gates radiate a traceable signature, and more gate activity
  → more misdials. Retune: N driven throats on one world compound — a contested world
  flares up the visibility ladder while the fight runs, drawing crashers within the
  contest's Potential band. Escalation becomes diegetic: fights attract company; quiet
  extractions stay quiet; loud wins are paid for in attention.
- Knob: findability/visibility (existing) — the flare is Potential-visibility applied to
  a world instead of a push. No new number.
- Trap-check: only frontier worlds flare (homes are single-throat, bilateral, off the
  random pool); crashers still pay their own dial and their own away reserve.
- Tunables: flare-vs-throat-count curve, visibility-band width.

### D. Misdial spill into contests — the E that ramps — [retune of FRONTIER-2 + TAME-1]

- Canon already: misdials arc open onto random frontier worlds; monster-spill exists;
  rising instability is the difficulty ramp. Retune: weight the random pool by strain,
  so worlds with many driven throats are where un-propped lanes arc open — dumping
  creatures (or an opportunist's free outbound lane) into the hottest fights. The E
  escalates as a fight drags: extract fast, or fight the planet too.
- "Outbound only, never invasion in" holds: homes were never in the pool, and a frontier
  world is already the contact surface — this is the existing misdial seen from the
  receiving end. Un-propped lanes still self-collapse (bounded, per gate-physics).
- Bonus: the game-life difficulty ramp (rising instability) now shows up where players
  actually are, not just as a background number.
- Tunables: strain weighting of the pool, spill composition per world rank.

### E. Dead houses on standing orders — the AI player — [extension of HOUSE-2]

- One line of fiction closes it: _the frontier ate the gater; the base's automation
  keeps running its last orders._ A dead house doesn't just defend — it keeps dialing:
  pushes into frontier worlds, taps holdings, hauls goods through hubs.
- That single extension makes AI exercise every already-designed contest surface as a
  third party: dead-house pushers appear in contested worlds (PvPvE at population 1),
  their haulers are ambushable at hubs (the bandit loop), their holdings are
  contestable, their pushes are visible at their Potential band. Nothing new to
  balance — Potential, visibility, away reserve, dome, muster cap all apply unchanged;
  reuse is HOUSE-2's whole point, extended from the noun to the verbs.
- **Hard rule (trap-check): an AI house never hunts player homes.** It collects no
  shares and assembles no coordinates; AI aggression lives only on the always-contestable
  surfaces (frontier, holdings, hubs). Anything else re-invents offline loss by robot
  and breaks the healthy/toxic line.
- Behind-the-curtain fit, at no new fiction cost: the selection engine wants pressure;
  where the arena is under-populated, the leftover automation _is_ the pressure.
- PvE-dodge guard extends naturally: rewards from beating AI actors sit below
  player-sourced equivalents at tier (same knob as the dead-house loot discount, #10).
- Tunables: standing-order repertoire per tier, AI-activity density per region,
  AI-vs-player reward discount.

### F. Recon probe through a listed lane — [option bank, catalogued]

- Spend less than a full dial to peek: world tags, gate count, current strain/flare.
  Committing a core to a contested world becomes an informed bet; AI houses use the same
  verb. Cheap adoption; pays the PvPvE decision loop and the scout archetype.
- Plausibility: if matter transits, a sensor packet transits — the cheapest payload.
- Tunables: probe cost band, what a probe can/can't see.

### G. Occupy-to-lock · buffer-wipe · control-device steal — [option bank, sharpest; defer]

- The tactical layer at the gate seam once fights exist: keep a target's line busy
  (siege denial), cut a tunnel to wipe in-transit forces (raid commitment), steal the
  console (stranded-not-wiped). All attacker/defender-symmetric, so AI houses can use
  them too. Defer until the aperture fight is proven fun — they deepen a loop that must
  exist first.

## Suggested order

- **E, then C/D** — the PvPvE engine and the greybox's low-population lifeline.
- **A and B** — cheap, high-leverage adoptions that give existing rules an in-world
  carrier.
- **F, then G** — after the aperture fight is proven.

## Deliberately not suggested

- New gate types or a second travel/arena system (one-cosmology call stands).
- Named AI factions, personalities, or politics (Builders-unknown + no speculative
  content).
- Any defender-side timer, window, or AI threat to sealed/unfound homes (rejected calls
  and the healthy/toxic line stand).
