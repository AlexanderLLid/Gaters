# Interface Trap - Mystery Phenomenon Options

Decision-support, not canon. Tracked as `questions.md` #25.

Goal: find Gaters' equivalent of _The Three-Body Problem_'s Dark Forest: a compact
phenomenon that makes the world rules feel inevitable, memorable, and mysterious.

## Recommendation

Use **the Interface Trap**.

> Any civilization that learns to read the Gate interface is already inside the test.

The Gate Council believes it decoded neutral Gate telemetry. In truth, the network may
have exposed a partial interface on purpose. The labels humans trust - claimable, tier,
Potential, route, listing, unstable, standing, safe - are useful tools, but also prompts
that make players, guilds, markets, and governments reveal how they behave under pressure.

The real-world frame is **mechanism design / revelation game**:

- A designer cannot directly see hidden traits.
- So the designer builds rules, costs, rewards, and categories.
- Self-interested actors then reveal private information through choices.

The danger flavor is **Goodhart pressure**:

- Once a metric becomes visible, actors optimize for it.
- Their optimization becomes new data.
- The system can escalate, retune, or expose the next layer.

## Why this fits Gaters

- **Claiming** becomes operator registration, not property magic.
- **Potential** becomes a pressure band, not only a strength stat.
- **Listings** become test-surface releases, not just patch notes.
- **Masks/UI** become human translations of a machine-facing interface.
- **The Gate Council** remains useful and honest enough; it decoded the layer the network
  allowed humanity to learn.
- **Aliens** can have incompatible readings of the same underlying interface.
- **PvP and guilds** are no longer arbitrary violence; they are the most legible way the
  system gets data about commitment, cooperation, betrayal, recovery, and scale.

## Story Spine

1. **The Builders could not solve continuity in a lab.**
   They needed to know which embodied minds can survive transfer, death, replacement,
   memory loss, social pressure, territory, and recovery.

2. **The probes built a playable interface.**
   The network had to be usable by species the Builders never met, so it exposed simple
   operational states instead of language: reachable, unsafe, claimed, stronger, farther,
   stable, unstable.

3. **The Council decoded the tutorial layer.**
   Human institutions mapped machine states into masks, guilds, Potential, listings,
   access standing, and claims.

4. **The decoded labels changed human behavior.**
   Guilds optimized Potential, chased listings, guarded claims, exploited safe/unsafe
   thresholds, and built a whole economy around the interface.

5. **That optimization is the test.**
   The network is not only measuring who wins. It measures what actors do after the rules
   become visible.

## Mystery Setup

### Early

- The Council UI feels official and reliable.
- "Claimable", "tier", "Potential", "listing", and "unstable" are treated as decoded
  facts.
- Claims, raids, and frontier lanes behave predictably enough that players trust them.

### Mid

- Some labels turn out to be interpretations, not truths.
- "Safe" means safe under a test condition, not safe forever.
- Potential predicts visibility, but high Potential also seems to attract stranger
  events.
- Alien-held Gates do not cleanly map to Council categories.
- New listings appear when human behavior crosses thresholds, not only when engineers
  finish "decoding" them.

### Late

- Players learn the Council did not crack a neutral operating system.
- It learned the interface the network wanted humans to learn.
- The interface is a tutorial, scoreboard, and experiment harness.
- The question shifts from "Who built the Gates?" to "What did our use of the interface
  teach the machine?"

## What It Explains

- **Why UI exists:** Gates expose operator telemetry; masks and Council systems translate
  it.
- **Why claiming exists:** claiming starts an operator record.
- **Why Potential exists:** Potential is the visible part of a pressure/eligibility model.
- **Why progression is gated by listings:** listings are controlled releases of new test
  surfaces.
- **Why hubs exist:** hubs are high-traffic junctions where the interface can observe
  exchange, coordination, monopoly, and conflict.
- **Why the Council is hands-off:** it profits from the same churn the machine wants to
  observe, without understanding the deeper purpose.

## Alternatives Considered

### The Proving Game

The Gates are explicitly arenas and claims mark contestants.

- Good: cleanest support for PvP, tiers, claims, Great Guilds.
- Bad: risks making the Builders feel like literal game designers.
- Use only as subtext, not surface canon.

### The Red Queen Network

Every decoded advantage forces escalation. You must keep adapting because everyone else
is adapting too.

- Good: strong progression and arms-race feel.
- Bad: less unique; explains escalation better than UI/mystery.

### The Dark Forest Variant

Every Gate use reveals you, so silence is safety and action is exposure.

- Good: already matches "reach equals exposure."
- Bad: too close to current surface thesis; does not explain Council decoding, UI, claims,
  or game-in-game mystery.

## Guardrails

- Do **not** say "the Builders made a video game."
- Do **not** make the Council fake or stupid. It decoded real, useful controls.
- Do **not** add a hidden morality score.
- Do **not** create a new visible meter if Potential, heat, claims, listings, and mask data
  already carry the idea.
- Do **not** reveal the whole truth early. The phenomenon should be inferred from label
  mismatches and escalating consequences.

## Canon Fold If Adopted

- `world.md#Gate Council`: the Council decoded a partial interface, not full control.
- `world.md#Behind the curtain`: the network uses interface categories to create a
  revelation game across individuals, guilds, species, and institutions.
- `systems.md#Mask energy`: masks translate Gate telemetry into human HUD.
- `systems.md#Potential`: Potential is the human-facing pressure/eligibility label.
- `systems.md#Claiming`: claiming starts an operator record; Council maps it to guild
  ownership.

One-line canon candidate:

> The Gate Council learned the interface the network allowed humanity to learn; every
> decoded label is both a tool and a test.
