# Avatar Origin — Options Report

_Decision-support, not canon. Generated from a 12-solution fan-out, each stress-tested by a skeptic. Nothing here is locked._

**The question:** what _is_ a Gaters avatar, where does its body come from, how is your Earth-bound mind connected to it — consistent, low-fantasy, justifying the bare "bow-and-stone" start and "why not just send a robot", ideally with an **evolution** angle.

## Headline: the 6 invariants (the real finding)

Every solution, regardless of flavor, hit the **same fixable holes**. These six are therefore hard constraints — design to them and the rest is taste. The per-option scores (4–6/10) all reflect "strong idea, needs these standard fixes," not weak ideas.

1. **Two arrival modes, not one.** Canon's deepest page already says the **dome lets matter (bodies + gear) cross a stable Gate**. So "no body crosses, only a signal/seed" is **only true for dome-less Rifts**. Split it: **Rift = cast-and-bare** (body grown on-site), **Gate = carried-and-equipped** (real matter transit). This one split makes bare-start, naked Rifts, _and_ the Gate-vs-Rift difference fall out — and stops contradicting [[gate-physics|Gate Physics]]. Raiding/trade/extraction stay unchanged.
2. **The link rides the network, not the throat.** A Rift collapses in seconds; you pilot for minutes-to-hours. Real-time control can't ride the open throat — it rides the persistent consciousness-link channel (the same conceit that already lets "comms pass a sealed Gate"). Anchor lives in the avatar.
3. **The mask is the one durable Earth-origin object — and it carries the coordinate-share.** A grown body is local matter that decomposes; but `SHARE-1` (the entire obscurity / home-finding engine) needs a physical fragment **looted off the dead avatar**. Put the share **in the mask**. The mask is also the link anchor and the life-support/HP (already canon). One object, three jobs — which nearly every skeptic independently demanded.
4. **Recall stays a committed cost.** No free re-lock / cheap round-trip on a parked body — re-locking costs a fresh cast or a cooldown. Otherwise "be online elsewhere to protect home for free" returns — the exact defender tax the design exists to kill.
5. **Builder-agnostic.** The casting/growing tech is **found / reverse-engineered / emergent** — never "the Builders intended avatars." Feedstock is operator-base biotech or Rift debris, never an assumed Builder service. (The Builders are unknown by design.)
6. **No spawn-camp window; cap penned bodies.** A "weak while it grows" arrival window reopens the doorstep kill the dome exists to prevent — so growth/respawn happens **behind your own field** (operator base). Any parked/penned roster bodies stay **inert and under the muster cap**.

## The options — 4 archetypes

The 12 collapse into four real families. The genuine open choice is **what the body physically is**; the spine (invariants above) is shared. Evolution-strength and fantasy-level noted per family.

### A. Grown-to-a-template (printed / cloned)

> _bioprint · transporter-copy · seed-cell · vat-logistics_

- **Body =** grown on-site (or vat-stocked) from a stored **genome/blueprint**; mind streamed in. Original never leaves Earth.
- **Evolution =** edit the recipe **between runs** — bank traits (cold hide, denser muscle, extra limbs, non-humanoid frames) and re-print an upgraded body. ARK mutation tree on your own lineage, as a data-driven unlock + print-cost sink.
- **Fantasy:** lowest. Classic SF transporter/clone. **transporter-copy** resolves identity cleanest ("only avatars die" is literally true — exactly one of you).
- **Best for:** evolution as a **loadout/tech-tree between casts**, and the cheapest engineering (respawn = print job; Empty/Solo/Contested maps to banked-seed / one-body-tick / full-instance).
- **Watch:** the copy/identity question (state plainly: the mind is never copied, the body is slaved actuation); raider-arrival-on-enemy-soil needs the two-mode split to resolve.

### B. Seeded-and-evolved organism (live, in-run growth) — _best evolution payoff_

> _seed-evolve · adaptive-blank · coherence-shell · wildcard (loom-seed)_

- **Body =** you arrive as near-nothing (a cell/seed) and the body **grows and adapts in real time**, physically sculpted by the world (heat → insulation, high-G → dense bone, toxic → filter organs).
- **Evolution =** the literal spine and **in-run**: Solo-Leveling from-nothing climb; biomass = avatar-XP; remembered forms = mind-XP (re-grow fast to your high-water-mark, slow beyond into new mutations). Each **new world type = a new build for near-free** (content multiplier).
- **Fantasy:** low-medium. One honest conceit — directed/accelerated morphogenesis — tag it and **cap/pace it** (regrow = reassembly of a remembered form from stored biomass, not free shapeshifting).
- **Best for:** making "evolve" the **core feel**, the from-scratch fantasy diegetically airtight (you literally start as a sprout), and a rich biomass resource sink.
- **Watch:** pace body-assembly over the respawn timer (no spawn-camp window, invariant 6); gate the trait ratchet so it doesn't reinstate a snowball.

### C. Mask-kernel — _best engineering economy_

> _mask-kernel_

- **Body =** the **mask is a small found precursor kernel** that accretes/grows a body around itself from local matter to an unlockable pattern.
- **Why it stands out:** it **natively satisfies invariants 2 & 3** — the kernel _is_ the link anchor and the share carrier (the two things that broke the most other solutions). One conceit unifies mask + link-anchor + HP + share-drop. Cleanest data model: avatar = kernel-id + pattern + deltas.
- **Evolution =** unlockable **shell patterns** (chitin, gills, heavier frame) + biome-biased accretion; mind-XP unlocks patterns, re-accretion re-levels.
- **Fantasy:** low. **Best for:** treating the kernel as the spine and bolting flavor A or B on top.
- **Watch:** the kernel itself must be **non-lootable** (only the shell + gear drop, kernel re-anchors home) or death over-punishes the roster.

### D. Living creature / symbiote (folds taming in)

> _symbiote · beast-pilot · native-host_

- **Body =** the avatar **is a creature** you grow/bond/breed — **merges taming and the avatar into one system** (tamed creatures = roster bodies / breeding stock / pen labor).
- **Evolution =** strongest ARK loop: feed/breed/mutate/graft a juvenile up its biome tier into an apex form. The creature-genome data model (base + deltas) is a cheap roster row.
- **Fantasy:** low-medium. **symbiote (6/10)** is the cleanest (one no-robots conceit: only living wetware hosts the imprint; mask integrates perfectly). **beast-pilot (5/10)** folds taming fully but trips the no-deep-breeding call + the muster cap. **native-host (4/10, broken)** — riding wild local animals — is the weakest: body-snatch read + the share-supply collapses (a wild animal can't carry your home address).
- **Best for:** killing a whole separate taming subsystem and getting ARK growth "for free."
- **Watch:** bind breeding to the **lossless registry already decided** (no upkeep regression); cap penned defenders under the muster cap.

## Recommendation

**Spine = C (mask-kernel). Flavor = B (live evolved organism). Apply all 6 invariants.**

Concretely:

- The **mask is a found precursor kernel** — the one durable Earth-origin object. It holds the **pilot link anchor**, the **life-support/health** (mask-energy), and your **current coordinate-share** (looted off your corpse → `SHARE-1` satisfied). Builder-agnostic: found/reverse-engineered, never "intended."
- **Arrival, two modes:**
  - **Rift (no dome):** nothing solid crosses; the network casts your pattern to the kernel, which **seeds and grows a bare body from local matter** → bare start is _structural_, not a rule (you sent a cell).
  - **Gate (domed):** you cross **carried-and-equipped**, gear and all → trade/raiding/extraction untouched.
- **Pilot link** rides the persistent consciousness-link channel (comms-pass-a-sealed-Gate conceit), not the collapsing throat → real-time control survives a Rift.
- **Why not robots:** only **living, self-organizing tissue** can host a human mind's real-time imprint (one conceit); a machine has nowhere to bind the kernel and no living substrate; and the Coalition charters **accountable humans, not autonomous drones**. (Don't lean on "transit shears hardware" — the dome defeats that.)
- **Evolution (the fun part, in-run):** the grown body is the substrate. Avatar-XP = the body adapts/mutates to the world and to use; mind-XP = permanent growth patterns you re-grow into; biome-sculpted builds = your roster (templates-on-file). Cap the morphogenesis conceit (reassembly of remembered forms), tag it honestly.
- **Death/recall:** death destroys the grown body, **kernel drops as loot carrying the share**, mind re-anchors home, regrow at the operator base (behind your field — no spawn-camp). Recall = drop the link, body + shell abandoned, **kernel re-anchors with you** (committed cost, not free re-lock).

This is essentially **seed-evolve + mask-kernel + seed-cell merged**, with the invariants applied. It makes bare-start structural, no-robots single-conceit, evolution the spine, fantasy low, Builders untouched, and breaks **no** balance lever.

## The one decision for you

The spine and invariants hold for all three; the real pick is **flavor of body**:

- **Printed/cloned (A)** — evolution as a between-runs tech tree; lowest fantasy; cleanest identity.
- **Live evolved organism (B)** — evolution in-run and literal; the strongest "evolve" feel; one capped conceit. _(my rec, matches your "evolve angle")_
- **Creature/symbiote (D)** — folds taming into the avatar; strongest ARK loop; a bit more fantasy.

Everything else (mask-kernel spine, two arrival modes, share-in-mask, network-link, recall cost) I'd treat as settled by the convergent analysis unless you disagree.
