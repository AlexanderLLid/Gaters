# World Spine — the core model

The synthesized core loop and cosmology, decided in design. **This is the current source of truth for the avatar / Gate / selection model** and supersedes the older "projection" framing. Page-by-page reconciliation of the canon pages is still pending (see _Supersedes_ at the bottom).

Tags: [decided] = current call + why; [tentative] = leaning; [open] = tracked in [[open-questions|Open Questions]].

## The one theme everything serves

- **Survival of the fittest, where the _gene_ is the unit of selection — not the gater.** [decided]
- The Gates and the worlds form a **galaxy-scale natural-selection engine**. You are a disposable **vehicle**; what is selected, persists, and propagates is the **genetic pattern**.
- This is the dark undercurrent under a capitalist gold rush (see _Two layers_). The horror reveal: _it was never you they wanted — the gene is._

## Eligibility — the awakening [decided]

- Players are **humans whose DNA is _compatible_ with the Gate tech**. Most people can't gate; a rare few can. "Awakening" = discovering you're compatible.
- Compatibility is **species-agnostic** — the Gate reads the pattern, not the species. So **aliens with compatible genes slot into the same arena later at zero extra machinery** (roadmap; multi-species stays scope-deferred for now).
- _Why some humans are compatible_ is the **central mystery** — do not answer in-game (see [[builders-truth|Builders' Truth]] for the behind-the-curtain candidate). Players keep "am I Builder-blooded?" as a live question.

## The avatar — a backed-up clone (NOT a projection) [decided]

- Your **mind is backed up**; your **body is a clone grown from your genome**. The clone is genuinely present and mortal.
- **Only clones die.** On death you re-decant from backup at an anchor. Your continuity — the **evolving genome/backup** — is never lost. This _is_ "selection without extinction," now literal Selfish-Gene: the vehicle dies, the gene improves and continues.
- **Two-layer XP** maps onto this exactly:
  - **Mind-XP** = the backup/genome: permanent, account-wide knowledge + **banked adaptations**. Never lost.
  - **Avatar-XP** = the fresh clone body: re-levels fast to your evolved **high-water-mark**, lost on death.
- This replaces the old "your real body stays on Earth and you pilot a projected avatar." No projection, no remote-piloting link to maintain.

## Grown from local matter → the bare/basic start [decided]

- Bodies grow **on-site from local biomass** (Gate-worlds are living — see [[builders-truth|Builders' Truth]] for why). Cheap, renewable, automated.
- **Why you start with almost nothing:** a body grows _empty-handed_ — gear is manufactured matter, not grown. The asymmetry that survives "the patron is rich enough to clone you, why not arm you":
  - **A body is near-free** (copy a backup + grow from free local biomass). **A weapon is finite refined matter** that must be made and is **lost to your killer on death** — an infinite leak, especially since most gaters don't come back.
  - So the **issued charter kit = cheap, durable, reusable** (a blade, a spear, a pick). **Never consumable/advanced** (guns/ammo) — wasteful on the dead, and useless without resupply (a gun is a club once the magazine's empty on a raw world).
- **Looted/bought advanced weapons are temporary power spikes** — good until the ammo/charge runs out, then dead weight until you build the supply chain to feed them.
- **Tech tree = a supply-chain / bootstrap climb, not a knowledge gate.** Knowing ≠ having: your genome may _know_ how to make a rifle, but a schematic isn't a factory. You climb stone → smelt → metal → machines.
  - **Visible tree = human know-how**, freely shared (no Coalition hoarding — kills the "if it wanted us to survive it'd share" objection).
  - **Locked tree = Builder tech** you _discover and reverse-engineer in the world_ — discovery-gated, not permission-gated. The Coalition can't give what it hasn't decoded. (This is the "accidental discovery" engine and the reason to explore.)

## The Gate — power, tether, map border (two-tier energy) [decided]

- A Gate projects a **field** (ambient energy):
  - **Ambient field = free local power.** Runs your facility, sustains your mask. Costs no scarce fuel → **holding a world has no upkeep** (kills the defender tax).
  - **Power cores (scarce) = only needed to _open/fire_ a Gate for travel** (dial out, raid, ship). Cores stay the scarcity that drives conflict.
- **The mask is avatar tech keyed to draw the stabilizing field from _any_ Gate in range** (not just yours — or you couldn't travel). The clone body isn't fully self-sustaining; the mask keeps it knit by tapping the nearest Gate.
  - **Beyond all Gate range → the body fails.** This is the **tether and the map border** — subtle (your tech's dependence on a Gate), not a magic death-zone.
- **Attunement decides home vs hostile:**
  - **A Gate you've claimed (your soil):** full top-up, unlimited time.
  - **A foreign/unclaimed Gate:** the mask functions but can't fully replenish → you're on a **depleting away-clock** (the raider clock). Clock length is set by your own mask, not the local Gate (un-pumpable by the enemy).
- **Map bounding (the server win):** the playable space is the **field bubble** around each Gate; beyond it is an **ungenerated death zone**. The universe is a **network of bubbles** joined by Gates/Rifts — no open space between to generate. **Field radius = the scaling dial** (grow the world as capacity allows).

## The dome — no-build plaza, open breach [decided]

- An active Gate raises a **dome** (inner radius) that **destroys/forbids player-built structures** (senses construction, not terrain), shields transit, and **prevents spawn-camping**. The Gate sits in an open plaza; **you build in the ring** between dome edge and field edge.
- **Two radii = two independent dev knobs:** dome radius (the combat plaza) and field radius (map size).

## Claiming a Gate — imprint your DNA [decided]

- **Claim = imprint your genetic signature** (a blood sample / the Gate reads your genome). It does three things at once:
  1. **Attunes** the Gate to you → full sustain (home soil).
  2. Makes it your **respawn forge** — keyed to your DNA, the Gate grows _your_ clones from local matter. This _is_ the cloning facility.
  3. Marks **ownership** until overwritten.
- **The imprint is your current, evolving genome** — claimed Gates grow your **latest evolved form**. Survive harsh worlds → bank adaptations → re-imprint → your respawns keep up with what you've become.
- **Raiding = overwrite the imprint** (stamp your DNA over theirs) to seize a Gate. Folds into the existing re-key mechanic.
- **Keep two keys separate:** the **bio-imprint** (whose soil / who it grows) vs the **secret coordinate** (findability / obscurity). Re-key the address to stay hidden without changing your genome.

## Anchors, the facility, and the first-jump knife-edge [decided]

- **Anchor = where you respawn.** Newcomers respawn at the **Safe Core hub** (baseline, always available — you never truly die).
- **Build a forward facility on a claimed Gate** → respawn _there_ to hold the world. It runs off that Gate's ambient field.
- **The first-jump hook:** Rift in to a **random foreign Gate** → the away-clock is already ticking → **race to claim it** (imprint + facility) before the clock or a rival ends your run. Succeed = it's your soil, forward respawn. Fail = bounce to the hub, world lost. **The away-clock _is_ the establishment timer.**
- **Die-a-lot is fine** (the Rust loop): death bounces you to your last anchor (hub or forward facility), costing gear + position + the run — **never your existence.** Hub = the beach; your facility = the sleeping bag/cupboard; raiding = breaking the facility to evict + loot.

## Rifts — why they exist [decided]

- A **Gate** is a _controlled, propped, directed_ wormhole (core-held, dialed to an address). A **Rift is the same phenomenon uncontrolled** — and that's not coincidence.
- The **Wake left the network unstable.** An active Gate channels enormous energy through that destabilized fabric, and the strain **arcs** — tearing open spontaneous, unpropped throats nearby. (A frayed high-voltage line throwing sparks.)
- One cause explains every Rift property: **unpropped** (collapses on a countdown), **undirected** (dumps you at a _random_ Gate — your unknown destination), **clustered near Gates** (where the energy concentrates).
- **You dive a Rift → emerge at a random _unclaimed/frontier_ Gate, one-way** (can't return the way you came). **Sealed/claimed homes are off the random pool** → preserves obscurity (no stranger dropped in your living room).
- **More Gate activity → more Rifts** (more strain). Network-wide instability **ramps over time** = the difficulty curve and the visible symptom of the central mystery.

## The factions [tentative]

- **The Coalition administrates the Gate network** (and/or the charter) and takes a cut. **Hands-off — not a schemer.** The selection isn't its plan; it's the **system's nature**. The Coalition may not even understand what the machine is doing.
- **A separate company cracked the body-growth/clone tech** (the "hidden tech"). You're a **self-employed prospector** using both — beholden to neither. _(Second faction is optional weight; keep if it earns its place.)_
- _This supersedes_ the United Gate Coalition's "secretly forging humanity" motive — selection moves from the Coalition's scheme to the Builders' machine.

## Two layers — what you play vs what's true

- **Surface (what you play):** a capitalist survival gold rush — claim Gates, build, raid, climb, get rich, work for yourself. (Rust/EVE/Valheim/Solo-Leveling.)
- **Hidden (what you uncover):** the gold rush _is_ the selection pressure. The competition you think you're winning is the machine breeding the fittest gene. **You're livestock that thinks it's a prospector.** Matches the "frontier gold-rush with cosmic unease" tone.
